Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the first mission to land humans on the Moon. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above. This mission marked a historic achievement for humanity and opened the door to lunar exploration.

Voyager

Launched to explore the outer planets, Voyager 1 became the first spacecraft to enter interstellar space. It continues to send valuable data about the boundary of our solar system more than 45 years after launch.

Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Telescope revolutionized astronomy by capturing detailed images of deep space. It helped scientists measure the universe's expansion and discover galaxies billions of light-years away.

Chang'e 4

Chang'e 4 was the first spacecraft to successfully land on the far side of the Moon. It deployed the Yutu-2 rover to study the lunar surface and conduct unique scientific experiments never done before.

SpaceX Falcon 9

SpaceX's Falcon 9 became the first orbital-class rocket capable of landing and being reused. This innovation reduced launch costs and made spaceflight more sustainable and frequent.

Mars Rover Curiosity

Curiosity landed on Mars to study its surface and atmosphere. It discovered evidence that Mars once had water and could have supported microbial life, advancing the search for past life on the Red Planet.

Parker Solar Probe

The Parker Solar Probe is the first mission to "touch" the Sun's outer atmosphere. It collects data on solar winds and magnetic fields to help scientists understand how the Sun affects space weather and Earth.

James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Telescope is the most advanced observatory ever built. It looks deeper into space than any telescope before, revealing the first galaxies and studying exoplanets in incredible detail.

Cassini-Huygens

Cassini explored Saturn and its moons for 20 years. It discovered water-ice geysers on Enceladus and sent the Huygens probe to Titan, revealing a world with rivers and lakes of liquid methane.

Perseverance Rover & Ingenuity Helicopter

The Perseverance rover is exploring Mars to search for ancient life and collect rock samples. It carried the Ingenuity helicopter — the first aircraft to fly on another planet — marking a new era of aerial exploration on Mars.
